Correlation Heatmaps

Heatmap 1: This heatmap shows the correlation between various factors in the dataset. Darker colors indicate stronger correlations. For example, we can see a strong correlation between reading scores and writing scores.

Heatmap 2: This heatmap focuses on the correlations between demographic factors and math scores. Notice the significant correlations between parents' education levels and math scores.

Heatmap 3: This heatmap illustrates the correlations between different types of scores. It reveals that math, reading, and writing scores are highly correlated, suggesting that students who perform well in one area tend to perform well in others.

Heatmap 4: This heatmap shows the relationship between the number of siblings and student scores. It appears that having more siblings is negatively correlated with higher scores, indicating potential resource dilution.

Heatmap 5: This heatmap highlights the correlations between weekly study hours and scores. Higher weekly study hours are positively correlated with better scores, emphasizing the importance of consistent study habits.

Heatmap 6: This heatmap explores the impact of transportation means on scores. Students who walk or cycle to school tend to have slightly higher scores compared to those who use other transportation methods.
